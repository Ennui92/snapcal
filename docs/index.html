Point. Shoot. Eat.

SnapCal counts your calories from a single photo. No searching, no barcode scanning, no typing grams into little boxes. Log a meal in the time it takes to photograph it.

Android now, iOS when it earns it. Install by opening the APK on your phone.

📸Open, tap onceThe app opens straight into the camera. One tap logs the meal, then go eat it.
🤖AI does the countingIn the background, Gemini vision names the food, sizes the portions and adds up the calories.
🎯You see the lineA daily budget from your one-time setup. Under it, weight comes off. The ring keeps the score.

Built for zero effort

Every calorie app dies of too much typing. SnapCal's whole design is making the log entry cost nothing, so you actually keep doing it.

🖐️Your hand is the ruler

Put your hand next to the plate and the AI uses it for scale. Calibrated once during setup with your palm-to-fingertip length.

📦Remembers your products

That chocolate bar, that soda can: recognized once, stored on your phone, reused instantly. People eat the same things, so repeat logging is consistent and fast.

🎚️"I only ate half"

A big slider adjusts any entry to 0 to 100% eaten in two seconds. Items can be tweaked, removed or added if the AI missed something.

💬Gentle nudges

If the day is running hot by noon, SnapCal quietly suggests a lighter dinner. You pick how firm it should be, from off to strict.

☕️Everything counts

Meals, snacks, coffee, juice, that beer. Anything you can photograph, it can estimate: food, drink or a full tray.

📈Honest history

Days on budget, net calorie balance and the estimated effect on your weight, shared as text whenever you feel like bragging.

Your food diary is nobody's business

SnapCal has no account, no cloud database and no analytics. Your entire history lives in a database on your phone; export it as JSON any time. Photos are sent once for AI analysis and stored nowhere else. We could not sell your data even if we wanted to. We do not have it.

Try it on your next meal

Free. Installs in seconds. Setup is four friendly questions, then you never fill a form again.
