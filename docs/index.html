01 Open and shoot

Where other photo apps get it wrong

Guessing calories from a flat photo is mostly a guess about portion size, and that is where these apps miss badly. SnapCal fixes it with two things nobody else does.

Scale Your hand is the ruler

Measure your palm to fingertip once during setup. Put your hand in the shot and the AI has a known measurement to size the plate against, instead of guessing from nothing.

Memory The same food, the same number

Recognise that chocolate bar or soda can once and it is saved on your phone. Next time it is reused instantly, so your usual lunch does not cost different calories on Tuesday than it did on Monday.

Correction "I only ate half"

One big slider takes any entry from 0 to 100% eaten. Items can be edited, removed, or added if the AI missed the olive oil.

Honesty A budget with the maths shown

Your daily line comes from your own BMR and goal, worked out once at setup. Connect Strava or Health Connect and a hard workout raises the line for that day.

Restraint Nudges, not nagging

If a day is running hot by noon it quietly suggests a lighter dinner. Once. You choose how firm it gets, including off.

Everything Food, drinks, the lot

Coffee, juice, a beer, a full tray. Anything you can photograph it will estimate, in English, Ελληνικά, Deutsch, Español or Français.

Your food diary is nobody's business

No account. No cloud database. No analytics. Your entire history lives in a database on your phone and you can export the whole thing as JSON whenever you like. Photos go out once to be analysed and are stored nowhere else. We could not sell your data if we wanted to. We do not have it.
